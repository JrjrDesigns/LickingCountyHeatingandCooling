Frequently Asked Questions

HVAC Questions for Licking County Homeowners

How much does furnace repair cost in Newark, Ohio?

Furnace repair costs in Newark and Licking County typically range from $150 to $600 depending on the part and labor involved. Simple fixes like a dirty flame sensor or a thermostat replacement are on the lower end. A failed heat exchanger or blower motor can run higher. We always provide a free estimate before any work begins.

How often should I have my HVAC system serviced in Ohio?

We recommend a furnace tune-up every fall before heating season and an AC tune-up every spring before cooling season. Ohio winters put real stress on heating systems, and annual maintenance is the best way to prevent unexpected breakdowns and keep energy bills low.

Do you offer emergency HVAC service in Heath and Granville?

Yes. We offer 24/7 emergency HVAC service throughout Licking County, including Heath, Granville, Pataskala, Utica, Buckeye Lake, Hebron, Johnstown, and New Albany. Call anytime — day, night, weekends, or holidays.

How long does HVAC installation take in Licking County?

A standard furnace or air conditioner replacement typically takes 4 to 8 hours for a single technician. A full system replacement (both heating and cooling) usually takes one full day. We schedule most installations within a few days of your estimate.

What HVAC brands do you service in Newark, Ohio?

We service all major HVAC brands including Carrier, Lennox, Trane, Rheem, Bryant, York, American Standard, Goodman, Daikin, and more. Whether your system is 5 years old or 25 years old, we can diagnose and repair it.

Is it better to repair or replace my furnace?

A common rule of thumb is the '5,000 rule': multiply the age of your furnace by the repair cost. If the result exceeds $5,000, replacement is usually the better value. If your furnace is under 10 years old, repair almost always makes sense. We'll give you an honest recommendation either way.

Do you serve Buckeye Lake and Hebron?

Yes. We serve Buckeye Lake, Hebron, and all of southern Licking County. Seasonal and lake-area homes often need HVAC service at unexpected times — we're available year-round for all service needs.

What's the best time of year to replace an HVAC system in Ohio?

Spring and fall are ideal — demand is lower, scheduling is easier, and you have time to choose the right system without an active emergency. That said, we install systems year-round and can handle urgent replacements quickly when needed.
