Concrete & Paving Work in Johnstown

Johnstown's mix of established small-town homes and newer subdivisions built on former farmland creates two pretty different jobs. In-town properties often have mature landscaping and established grade around the house that we work around carefully. New subdivision properties usually sit on flatter, recently-graded lots, but the underlying soil can vary significantly across a former field, which affects how we plan the base.

Longer driveways are common on Johnstown's more rural, agricultural-adjacent lots — properties set back from the road on larger acreage. Longer runs change the base and drainage planning compared to a standard in-town driveway, and we scope those differently.

"Johnstown properties on former farmland can have real variation in soil type across a single lot — clay in one spot, looser fill in another, depending on how the field was used and graded before the subdivision went in. We don't assume the base is uniform just because the lot looks flat and new. A quick soil check during the estimate tells us where extra base depth or drainage work is actually needed."
