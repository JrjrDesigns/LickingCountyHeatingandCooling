Concrete & Paving Work in Granville

Granville homeowners tend to care more about how a driveway, walkway, or patio actually looks than we see in most other parts of the county, and it makes sense given the village's brick-street, historic-district character. We do more stamped and decorative concrete work in Granville than anywhere else we serve — stone and brick-pattern stamps that read as intentional next to century-old architecture instead of a plain gray slab that stands out for the wrong reasons.

Some Granville properties, especially near the historic downtown core, fall inside design review guidelines that can affect visible street-facing hardscape. We'll flag that early in the estimate if it's likely to apply to your property so there are no surprises.

"When customers ask us what we'd do with a front walkway on a Granville home near downtown, we usually steer them toward a stamped or exposed-aggregate finish rather than plain broom concrete. It costs more upfront, but on a property where the house itself has real architectural character, a plain slab out front actually undersells the home. It's one of the few places in the county where we lead with the decorative option instead of offering it as an upsell."
