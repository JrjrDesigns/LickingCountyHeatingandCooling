Concrete Patios & Walkways

Patios & Walkways That Fit Your Property

A concrete patio or walkway gets poured over the same properly compacted base as a driveway, just at a lighter design load, and it's usually a faster project — most standard patios and walkways are formed and poured in a single day, with a few more days for cure before furniture goes back on it.

We pour standard broom-finish for a clean, practical look, or step up to exposed aggregate or a stamped pattern for a more finished appearance. In neighborhoods with brick streets and older character — Granville in particular — we often recommend a stamped or stained finish that complements the existing streetscape rather than a plain gray slab.

"A lot of walkway calls we get in Licking County aren't new installs — they're replacing an old slab that's heaved a couple inches at one joint and become a trip hazard. If the rest of the walkway is solid, we can usually replace just the affected section rather than tearing out the whole thing. We tell people honestly when a partial fix will hold and when it won't."
  • Poured over compacted gravel base, same standard as driveways
  • Broom, exposed aggregate, or stamped finish options
  • Sidewalk section replacement for isolated trip hazards
  • Typically a 1-day pour on standard residential patios
