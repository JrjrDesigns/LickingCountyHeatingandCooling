Licking County Driveway Cost Guide

Driveway pricing depends on size, material, existing base conditions, and finish. Below are typical ranges we see across Licking County for both concrete and asphalt driveways. Every project is different — these are starting reference points, not a quote. We provide a free written estimate after seeing your actual site.

What Actually Drives the Price

Square footage is the obvious factor, but it's rarely the only one that moves a quote. Existing base condition matters more than most homeowners expect — removing old asphalt or concrete, or excavating and hauling away poor soil, adds real cost before any new material goes down. Drainage and grading can require additional work if water currently pools against the house or an existing surface. Access for equipment on tighter village lots can also affect labor time.

Repair & Maintenance Costs

Not every project is a full replacement. Isolated concrete crack repair typically runs a few hundred dollars; a full resurfacing overlay costs less than replacement but more than simple repair — see our concrete repair page for detail. Asphalt sealcoating is a routine maintenance cost, typically far less than the cost of premature replacement caused by skipping it — see our asphalt repair & sealcoating page.

Commercial Project Costs

Commercial concrete pads and asphalt parking lots are priced per square foot based on required base depth and surface thickness for expected traffic — they don't scale the same way residential driveways do. See our commercial concrete & asphalt page or call us directly for a project-specific estimate.

Getting an Accurate Quote

The only way to get a real number is an on-site visit — we check existing base condition, drainage, access, and your finish preference, then provide a written estimate. It's free, and there's no obligation to move forward.
