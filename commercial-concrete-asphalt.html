Commercial Concrete & Asphalt

Built for Daily Commercial Traffic

Commercial concrete and asphalt work carries a different set of requirements than residential driveways — thicker base sections, higher-strength mix designs, and layout that accounts for drainage, ADA-compliant accessible routes, and daily truck or drive-through traffic instead of occasional passenger vehicles.

We work with businesses across Licking County's commercial corridors — including Heath's industrial and retail areas along State Route 79 — on parking lot paving and resurfacing, loading dock aprons, equipment pads, dumpster pads, and sidewalk and ADA ramp work tied to site improvements.

"Commercial work gets scoped differently than a residential driveway from the first site visit. We're looking at drainage flow across the whole lot, where trucks will actually turn and back in versus where the plans say they will, and whether the base needs to be built for occasional heavy delivery trucks even if daily traffic is lighter. Getting that wrong shows up as ruts and failures within a year or two under real-world use, not the paper spec."
  • Reinforced concrete pads and aprons for commercial loads
  • Asphalt parking lot paving and resurfacing
  • ADA-compliant ramps and accessible route concrete
  • Drainage-aware layout for lots and loading areas
