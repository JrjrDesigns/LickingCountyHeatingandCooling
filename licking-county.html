Licking County, Ohio

One County, Very Different Properties

Licking County covers a lot of ground, literally and figuratively — a historic county seat in Newark, a well-preserved 19th-century village in Granville, a fast-growing suburban corridor in Pataskala, industrial and commercial development in Heath, and rural, agriculture-adjacent land around Hebron and Johnstown. We treat each property according to its actual site conditions rather than a one-size answer for the whole county.

That means checking existing base and soil conditions on every job, whatever the neighborhood — an older Newark lot with mature trees, a new-construction site in Pataskala, or a long rural driveway in Johnstown all need a different base plan, even though the finished concrete or asphalt looks the same on top.

"When people ask what a driveway costs in Licking County, the honest answer is: it depends more on your specific property than on which town you're in. We've poured driveways two miles apart that needed completely different base depth because of soil and drainage differences. We check the actual site every time instead of quoting off a countywide average."
